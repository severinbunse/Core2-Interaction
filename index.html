Course Description

This course exposes students to interactive concepts and techniques for applications. It is an investigation in the interface, the mechanism, the controls and the aims of interactive works. Students will learn how to design and develop interactive projects and understand how to undertake research and direct their thinking process from brainstorming to the final outcome. They will be given the tools to conceive, plan and develop an interactive system and they will become aware of the importance of design’s role in contemporary media.

Project 2: Oracle (due April 03)

In ancient times, oracles were believed to be mystical intermediaries, connecting people with all-knowing gods. Similarly, digital interfaces, including text fields, buttons, and checkboxes, serve as modern-day gateways, allowing users to retrieve specific information from an “all-knowing” AI.

Using Figma, design a digital interface that acts as a medium for communication between users and the “AI gods.” Your design should guide how and what information users can retrieve.




Requirements:

  • – The design should influence both the process and the type of information retrieved
  • – The final interface should demonstrate how the site works
  • – Use HTML elements such as input fields (e.g., text areas, dropdowns, sliders, buttons)
  • – Or event-driven interactions (e.g., keyboard presses, window resizing, mouse movement, microphone input)
